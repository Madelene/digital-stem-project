Projects

ALARM SYSTEM

  • Everyone has things they want to keep secure. Microchips provide a powerful new way of protecting valuables.
  • In this project, students will build a simple alarm system that will turn on a buzzer and LED when the alarm is triggered by a pushbutton

TRAFFIC LIGHTS

  • Not only do people tell microchips what to do, microchips can also tell people what to do. Therefore, it is important that they are sending the correct message.
  • In this project, students will learn the importance of the instruction sequence and timing functions while building their own traffic light from LEDs.

WASHING MACHINE

  • Microchips have the ability to take over many tasks that once needed to be done by hand. However, these tasks often require a set of instructions to be carried out in a specific order.
  • Students will learn more about giving instructions to microchips while they program their own washing machine.

STREET LIGHTS

  • Electric lighting has transformed the way people live. However, it is important not to waste energy.
  • In this project, students will make a street light that only turns on when required.

FRIDGE ALARM

  • The refrigerator is a very useful device that keeps our food from going bad.
  • Students will be making their own fridge alarm to help keep food fresh and save electricity.

THERMOSTAT

  • While no one likes being too hot or too cold, there are certain situations where keeping the temperature just right can mean the difference between life and death.
  • Students will use the temperature controller in this project to monitor and adjust the temperature, similar to an incubator.
